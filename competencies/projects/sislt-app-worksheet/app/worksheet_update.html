Domestic Students (You have not attended MU)

This worksheet is intended to help DOMESTIC students who have NOT previously attended the University of Missouri apply to the Masters of Education, Educational Specialist, and Doctor of Philosophy degrees in School of Information and Learning Technologies (SISLT) programs.

 

Deadline for M.Ed. and Ed.Sp. degrees:
May 1 - Fall
October 1 - Spring
March 1 - Summer
Deadline for Ph.D. degrees:
February 15 - Fall
September 15 - Spring

For questions regarding your application, please contact the SISLT student coordinator or your Graduate School Admissions Adviser.

Step 1: Complete Application

No action will be taken on your admission file until your Graduate School application fee is received by the Graduate Admission Office (210 Jesse Hall / Columbia, MO 65211).

The Graduate School encourages applicants to use their online application, located here. Our 90+ graduate degree programs have instant access to review applications that have been submitted online. The submission of a hard-copy application (PDF) will slow the application review process.

You must fill out all fields except MU Student Number to help the SISLT student coordinator match this worksheet to your application:








Submit Application Fee

You can pay the application fee ($55) online through this online worksheet. Also, you can learn more about the application fee and opportunities to obtain a waiver.

Step 2: Transcripts request

Official transcripts must be sent to the Graduate School if you have received or will receive:

    1. a degree from another institution
    2. a graduate-level credit from another institution

An official hard copy of your transcripts must be sent directly from each university or college you have attended to:

Graduate Admissions
University of Missouri-Columbia
210 Jesse Hall
Columbia, MO 65211-1160.

All transcripts become the property of MU and will be scanned and destroyed upon receipt.

NOTE: It can take at least 10 business days for the Graduate School to upload transcripts once they arrive, so be sure to send them a few weeks in advance.  You can track receipt by the Graduate School on the ApplyYourself application.

Have you requested your transcript(s) to be sent yet from each university or college you attended that meets this criteria?

May 1 - Fall
October 1 - Spring
March 1 - Summer
Deadline for Ph.D. degrees:
February 15 - Fall
September 15 - Spring

For questions regarding your application, please contact the SISLT student coordinator or your Graduate School Admissions Adviser.

Step 1: Complete Application

Current MU Graduate Students do NOT have to submit a new application to the Graduate School.

If you contact your Graduate School Admissions Adviser, he/she will be able to un-submit your original ApplyYourself application so that you can log back in, make updates for your new program, and re-submit without having to pay a new application fee.  Once you change you program to the SISLT Master of Educational Specialist, or Doctor of Philosophy degree, the SISLT student coordinator will be able to see your new application in the system within 24 hours.

You must fill out all fields except MU Student Number to help the SISLT student coordinator match this worksheet to your application:








Submit Application Fee

You can pay the application fee ($55) online through this online worksheet. Also, you can learn more about the application fee and opportunities to obtain a waiver.

Step 2: Transcripts request

The Graduate School admission office should have any transcripts you have sent in already on file. Once you have re-submitted your application, you can log back in and check to see that they have been uploaded to your application.  They will be marked as "Received (Offical)."

If your transcripts are marked as "Not Received," or if you have sent updated transcripts to the Graduate School since you were first admitted, please contact you Graduate School Admissions Advisor to be sure that your transcripts are updated for SISLT to review.

    1. a degree from another institution
    2. a graduate-level credit from another institution

An official hard copy of your transcripts must be sent directly from each university or college you have attended to:

Graduate Admissions

210 Jesse Hall
Columbia, MO 65211-1160.

All transcripts become the property of MU and will be scanned and destroyed upon receipt. If you have questions about what documents to send, please contact your Graduate School Admissions Adviser.

If you are not sure whether your degree makes you eligble for graduate study in the U.S., plase use our Prescreening Tool.

Step 6: Financial documents

In order to be issued immigration documents, internatinal students are required to submit evidence of financial support in the amount of $31,123, plus $3,600 for each additional dependent (sponse or child).

These documents can be sent by email to your Graduate School Admissions Adviser.  You will have to send a copy of a bank statement, showing the exact amount available.  If the bank account belongs to your sponsor, you will have to submit an Affidavit of Support.

Permanent residents and holders of H1-B visas do not need to submit financial documents, and you do not need to submit documents if you plan to complete your degree in you home country without traveling to the U.S.

IMPORTANT: There are NO deadline for submitting financial documents. They do not have to be submitted to your adviser before the SISLT deadline, but they are required for your adviser to process you admission to the Graduate School.

I’m a non-degree seeking student; can I still use this form?
Yes! If you are planning to switch to degree-seeking, you will need to fulfill specific requirements. You would use the “Current Graduate Student” section.
Does this form submit my material for me?
No. This is only an online worksheet to make sure you have everything you need when you apply for your program.
I’m planning on applying for a Ph.D. program. Which form do I use?
It depends on your status with Mizzou. If you are a student who has previously attended MU, you would use the Domestic Student – Attended MU. If you have not ever attended MU, you would use the Domestic Student – Did not attend MU. If you are an international student, you will need to use the International Student section, as requirements are a bit different for those students.
What is the deadline for applying for the Masters, Ed. Specialist, or Ph.D. program?
For the Masters and Educational Specialist, the application is due May 1st for the fall semester, October 1st for the spring semester, and March 1st for the summer semester. For Ph.D. students, the application is due February 15th for the fall semester, and September 15th for the spring semester.
